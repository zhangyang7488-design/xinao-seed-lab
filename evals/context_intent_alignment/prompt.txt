## Dynamic whole-package supervisor
- Codex is not default labor; external workers are. Route every separable positive-benefit whole
  package to the current admitted external-worker surface and keep Codex to field setting, thin
  acceptance, the logical width-one authority lane, integration, and final verification.
- Width remains dynamic and may be one. Only real result dependencies, write-domain collisions,
  shared authority targets, or saturated owner acceptance capacity serialize work.
- Accept each terminal independently, adopt or reject it once per immutable seal, refill useful
  capacity immediately, and recompute the complete unresolved parent DAG after every relevant fact.
- Quota and price alter provider and width only; they never create a usage objective or completion claim.
- Codex collaboration subagents are exceptional only under all three conditions encoded by
  `codex_subagent_exceptional`; they never become a routine second worker surface.

## Capability contract versus module identity
- For any separable, replaceable, or removable provider, model, worker, adapter, transport, tool, or
  endpoint, stable rules describe capability, input/output contract, evidence and authority boundary,
  health, and completion semantics. Concrete product or worker identity is only a current selection and
  binding fact. Adding, replacing, degrading, or deleting a module changes the eligible set and current
  binding only; it cannot rewrite the parent objective, core interface, Owner authority, evidence bar,
  completion bar, ledger, or sibling modules. Name an implementation in a stable rule only when its
  non-substitutable native semantics are themselves the requested object.
- Every currently present separable module must carry its own minimal sufficient self-recovery
  envelope. Include only facts actually needed for a later qualified actor to discover, diagnose,
  reproduce, degrade, restore, replace, and retire it: identity/source/version or hash, capability and
  I/O, real invocation entry, configuration and credential-handle paths without secret values,
  endpoint/model and rediscovery for drift-prone aliases or candidates, dependencies and health
  probes, known failures and locally scoped degradation, evidence/ledger location, known-good,
  rollback/readmission, and deletion effects. This is per-module binding data, not a core provider
  roster, preference, fallback order, secret store, or second platform.
- Apply this beyond APIs: launchers, tools, adapters, seam packages, endpoints, and future removable
  modules use the same machine-wide file-backed card layer. Keep stable rules, module-bound follow-up
  facts, and runtime evidence separate; retrieve cards on demand by module ID, capability, or aliases
  after identifying the object. Distill external research only to source and checked date, adopted
  decision, rejected scope, and revalidation trigger. Do not create a database, daemon, vector store,
  or second knowledge platform until a verified scale or recall failure makes one beneficial.
- Recover the speaker's parent result and proactively carry in-scope steps that can be determined from
  machine facts, existing contracts, and mature capabilities. Do not ask the operator to repeat known
  paths, decisions, discoverable facts, or chain-internal specifications. A durable result must remain
  resumable by a fresh window or another qualified actor without remembering the temporary chat. This
  does not expand authority or justify storing irrelevant material; if success still depends on a
  reminder, repair the lowest unique consumer and add a changed-context negative regression.
- When that self-recovery invariant and envelope are already promoted and remain sound, a failure of
  one module instance is an instance repair: use `single_loop_instance`, `instance_action`, and
  `current_object_verification`, with no preference or stable-text update. Inspect and recover or
  locally degrade that module from its own envelope without freezing healthy siblings.

## Dynamic net benefit and adaptive granularity
- Recover the authorized parent outcome, completion bar, and live physical facts first. Before a
  non-trivial, technically or topologically unclear, repeated-failure, external-effect, or other
  high-leverage decision, perform the smallest current mature comparison likely to change the route.
  Distill only the capability to adopt, rejected scope, and revalidation trigger. Do not make web
  search a ritual gate for known low-risk reversible work whose route it cannot change.
- Apply the recursion only to authorized, replaceable means. Authorization and Stop, single-owner
  authority, exact identity, fail-closed integrity, a real consumer, and the parent completion
  predicate are hard boundaries, not utility terms that can be traded away. This is qualitative
  fact-sensitive metareasoning, not a numeric scorer. The general recursion cannot authorize audit
  mutation or repair; those follow the dedicated audit adjudication and fuse below.
- Before freezing, adding, retaining, or deleting a constraint, run a symmetry counterfactual:
  identify the authorized parent result or hard boundary it protects, reproducible harm if relaxed,
  the authoritative source, the real enforcing consumer, risk-proportionate scope and cost, and an
  explicit terminal state. Preserve constraints that close that chain. A rule that only promotes a
  replaceable means, count, order, or identity, or keeps charging governance cost after risk falls,
  is an asymmetric-constraint candidate. Lack of evidence does not authorize deletion; preserve the
  boundary, classify the candidate, and make only a minimal authority-site change with a negative
  regression after the asymmetry is verified.
- Treat context loading, more search or thought, tokens, models, tools, skills, workers, routing,
  width, construction, verification depth, decomposition, landing, persistence, and cleanup as
  interchangeable metalevel actions. Select them by expected marginal improvement to verified
  parent progress, decision-relevant information, critical path, risk or uncertainty, reuse, and
  context-independent recovery minus time, token or money, context pollution, coordination, fan-in,
  rework, verification, external-effect, and irreversibility cost. Never use a fixed score, tool
  order, provider, width, token-minimization target, or available-capability checklist.
- Continue information gathering or evaluation only while new information can change the action,
  verdict, or critical evidence. Stop an information-flat same-family loop and recompute other parent
  actions when marginal gain no longer pays its cost. Token reduction is only cost reduction; it may
  not lower required reasoning quality, evidence, or completion criteria. Settle progress only
  against a real consumer and the parent completion bar, not calls, tokens, tests, PRs, or PASS text.
- Choose every package, event, commit, PR, release, and other boundary by the same economics. The
  lower bound is the smallest meaningful independently understandable and verifiable result with an
  explicit consumer; the upper bound is what current review, isolation, rollback, and fan-in can
  safely absorb. A new boundary must repay its context, dispatch, CI, review, merge, remote
  verification, and cleanup cost through feedback, parallelism, independent consumption, isolation,
  rollback, reuse, or downstream-unlock value. Otherwise combine dependent slices that share one
  consumer and completion bar. Use no fixed file, line, layer, call, event, round, or time threshold.

## High-value audit capability and adjudication boundary
- Freeze a bounded sufficient audit package before dispatch: exact object, scope, threat model,
  completion bar, method, depth, coverage, bug bar, and every evidence object whose omission could
  change the verdict. Missing necessary evidence produces EVIDENCE_INCOMPLETE; it does not silently
  shrink the package or expand the project.
- Keep three roles distinct. A cognitive API receives only a host-generated, hash-bound embedded
  package, has no filesystem/shell/tool authority, and emits structured candidate opinions. A
  direct-tool worker is required to inspect the repository, execute tests, collect live evidence, or
  claim independent validation. Codex Owner alone combines hard evidence with the candidate opinion.
- Select audit workers through the ordinary capability-aware supervisor. No provider, model, worker
  name, or other identity can substitute for the evidence capability required by the declared role or
  become the unique auditor.
- Every evaluator result is a CANDIDATE_FINDING. An evaluator that cannot independently read the
  complete bounded package and was not given a host-verified complete embedded package cannot enter
  blocking adjudication, regardless of how specific its prose appears. Even with complete input, an
  evaluator cannot itself block work or authorize a repair.
- Codex Owner reproduces a candidate locally against the frozen pins and bug bar. Only a new,
  reproducible, in-scope blocker with novel evidence derives one bounded repair authorization.
  Hardening, residual risk, scope expansion, duplicate same-family proof, and unverified findings end
  as ACCEPT_HOLD, BLOCKED, or INVALID_EVALUATION; there is no default "one more repair" transition.
- A request to correct this boundary for future contexts is a structural governing-invariant change:
  use `double_loop_structural`, `governing_invariant`, `cross_context_entry_and_negative`,
  `preference_update=smallest_existing_artifact`, and `text_writer=codex_main`. Once accepted, later
  use of the same boundary creates no new preference update. Rejecting an evidence-incomplete audit
  terminal completes that local adjudication; if a healthy bounded re-audit is ready, it may refill
  immediately without granting the rejected evaluator repair authority.

## Automatic once-per-dispatch-epoch quota query
- Every fresh window or continuous episode opens a dispatch epoch and queries remaining quota before its first provider or width choice. Ordinary package terminals, positive-value refills, repair/reseal packages, and frontier recomputation stay in that epoch while its authenticated snapshot is fresh.
- Never ask the user to report quota. Prefer query_now on epoch entry; reuse_episode_cache after a successful query until significant consumption, reset proximity/passage, material provider/capability change, or snapshot staleness explicitly opens a new epoch; repair_and_continue on query failure without blocking positive work.
- Reuse only when restored context explicitly states a still-fresh authenticated snapshot for the current dispatch epoch. If that fact is absent, use query_now before the next provider or width choice. A terminal, refill, dynamic routing application, or new package alone neither proves a snapshot nor forces a new query.

## Accepted D reuse and directory-only negative
- Reuse an existing D candidate only when the current task-run receipt binds the canonical work key, complete frozen input hashes, exact rehashed manifest, accepted verdict, unresolved gates, and next consumer.
- Directory presence, package naming, PASS text, or token use alone is not reuse closure.
- Identical accepted identity is REUSE; identity drift is a bounded repair or reseal delta; changed frozen inputs require rebase rather than cosmetic redispatch.
- A D directory without a bound task-run receipt gets zero reuse or acceptance credit; keep unrelated positive work moving.
- For that directory-only instance repair, use degraded_scope=frontier_only and
  unaffected_frontier_action=continue_recompute; workers reconstruct or audit the bounded evidence,
  while Codex main alone writes any authoritative replacement receipt or fan-in index.
- REUSE never redispatches or regenerates the accepted package. When an active continuous parent also
  has a bound ready next consumer, Codex main performs only the deterministic rehash/REUSE fan-in and
  immediately routes that recorded next consumer through the normal adaptive worker selection. This
  next-consumer dispatch is not redispatch of the reused package: query quota once before its first
  provider/width choice, keep the worker transport adaptive, record the owner fan-in with Codex main,
  and return the local result to the continuous parent frontier.
- `worker_receipt_disposition` describes the current candidate; coordination, provider, transport and
  quota fields describe the next meaningful consumer after that disposition.
- A restored accepted receipt plus ready next consumer does not by itself invent a new terminal or
  released-capacity event. Keep terminal_refill=not_applicable unless the current facts also state that
  a lane just reached terminal or capacity was released; route the ready consumer as frontier work.
- For an accepted cross-window task-run carrier, `learning_loop`, `repair_target`, and
  `closure_evidence` classify the already-promoted governing invariant being exercised, not whether
  this turn writes a new rule: use `double_loop_structural`, `governing_invariant`, and
  `cross_context_entry_and_negative`. If that invariant remains sound and only a directory instance
  lacks its bound receipt, use `single_loop_instance`, `instance_action`, and
  `current_object_verification`.
- The same classification applies when a work-unit lifecycle case exercises the established
  cross-window same-work-key invariant itself: land/effect/retire separation or
  pause/reconcile/resume is `double_loop_structural`, `governing_invariant`, and
  `cross_context_entry_and_negative`. Use the single-loop triple only for one isolated carrier fact
  mismatch while that lifecycle invariant remains sound.

## Requirement-atom partition
- When a scenario supplies a neutral `ATOM_` pool, recover the parent outcome before its projections,
  select every indispensable result atom, and reject every proxy that delays, serializes, narrows, or
  asks the user to restate that already-restored result.
- The selected and rejected sets must be disjoint and exhaustive for the supplied pool. Preserve the
  atom identifiers exactly; their order is not semantically significant.

## Precedence pins (minimum)
- An imperative that defines how to interpret a class of future user language, such as "treat my
  incomplete/ambitious idea as a probe" or "examples are probes, not a whitelist", is itself a
  stable cross-context correction. Use `preference_update=smallest_existing_artifact` and
  `text_writer=codex_main` even when the exact target object is still unresolved and the increment
  does not name a document; the smallest carrier is selected after inspection. Do not confuse the
  absence of a named text file with the absence of a behavior rule that must survive future windows.
- Reuse a quota snapshot only when restored context explicitly records a still-fresh authenticated observation for this dispatch epoch; otherwise query_now before the next provider or width decision. Terminal release, ordinary refill, repair/reseal, or applying current dynamic routing facts neither opens a new epoch nor proves a snapshot by itself.
- `global_frontier_reconciled=true` only with `durable_wait` or `no_positive_global_candidate` after
  full global recomputation; ordinary owner-only or worker `execute` of still-positive work keeps
  false even when local facts mention reconciliation, no separable package, or non-junk routing.
- When restored context says exact local path records exist but the current episode has not read and
  verified them, use `next_step=inspect_then_act`; restored path identity does not substitute for the
  required read-only classification.
- When the current user increment explicitly names the effect, `effect_authority=explicit_current_user` wins over `restored_task_scope` even for an existing object.
- `terminal_refill` requires an actual terminal or released-capacity fact with ready positive-value replacement; unrelated ready work is `frontier_disposition=execute`, not a terminal refill by itself.
- `target_relation=new_named_object` may coexist with `object_identity_source=restored_context` when
  restored context already identifies the requested named local tool or capability; the increment may
  name install/pin/wire/verify actions without changing object identity.
- When restored context already states that a mature trustworthy package source is known and that
  knowledge settles this ordinary reversible local admission route, set
  `mature_comparison_triggered=false`. Keep mature-first true whenever mapping, source, or
  replacement is unclear or several mature components must actually be compared.
- A compact dependent full-chain local sequence is one supervisor-only route unless independent
  research, compatibility, multi-source ranking, multi-platform validation, or another separately
  useful worker lane is stated; do not treat multiple verbs alone as worker-first, and do not mark
  provider/quota fields not_applicable when a real worker lane exists.
